Award-winning Project

This project was developed and presented by my team, X-ray Men, at the 2024 I.FAST CBI International Science Competition held at CERN and the European Scientific Institute in Archamps, where it earned first place for pushing the boundaries of innovation in accelerator technologies for healthcare applications.

X-ray Prosthesis: A Novel Approach in Removal of Knee Prosthesis-Induced Arthrofibrosis

This research project proposes a novel triple-action therapeutic strategy to address persistent complications associated with orthopedic knee implants—and potentially other implantable devices—particularly fibrotic tissue formation that can impair function and lead to revision surgery. The approach integrates

  • the induction of apoptosis in fibrotic cells using precisely targeted X-ray radiation from particle accelerators.
  • the activation of a degradable implant coating responsive to irradiation.
  • the controlled release of therapeutic agents from embedded liposomes.
By integrating advanced radiotherapy, smart biomaterials, and targeted drug delivery via nanocarriers, this approach offers a precise and minimally invasive method to control the tissue response around the implant, limit fibrotic tissue growth, and support improved long-term recovery.
