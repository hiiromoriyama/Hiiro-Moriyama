Strings Within

Your shining dewdrop kills the silent tide,
Your lighthouse splashes light when grim days clash,
Of what use, for the ocean’s seed has died?
Your clues don't fuse when doubt and reason crash,
Your spyglass sees not ships, but white canoes,
O’ marionette, control me with your strings,
Your voiceless noise propounds a hostile truce,
And every doubt you have will cling, it stings!
Imperishable fire that can’t ignite,
Am I the slave without and you the rule?
Am I divine or just a parasite?
Am I a cubic wheel and you the fuel?
 So then, must I bend to the fool therein?
 So drowning surface, where are you within?
