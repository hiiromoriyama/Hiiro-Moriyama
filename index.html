Resume

Driven and ambitious Japanese physicist with a multidisciplinary foundation in physics and engineering, specializing in Ultrafast X-ray Optics and their application to capture rapid processes in areas such as fusion research, aerospace engineering, and medical imaging. Achievements include winning a gold medal in an International Science Competition at CERN and gaining valuable experience at world-renowned research facilities like CSIC, DESY, European XFEL, Stanford University and SLAC - US Department of Energy. Passionate about promoting collaboration across research teams and connecting academia with industry, with a long-term vision of launching a startup tech company.

Professional Experience

Research Scientist

Dec. 2024 - Present

Spanish National Research Council, Madrid, Spain

I am currently working with the MESO group at CENIM-CSIC, within the Department of Physical Metallurgy, under the supervision of Dr. Ricardo Fernández Serrano and Dr. Gaspar González-Doncel. Our work focuses on a multimodal approach (machine learning, experimental methods, and mathematical modelling) to X-ray and neutron diffraction analysis of residual stresses in materials, with the goal of developing eco-sustainable components for the automotive, aerospace, and medical industries.

Research Intern

Apr. 2024 - Jun. 2024

Okinawa Institute of Science and Technology, Okinawa, Japan

I worked under Dr. Pinaki Chakraborty and Dr. Julio M. Barros in the Fluid Mechanics Unit, where I investigated laminar and turbulent 2D flows using various imaging techniques.

Research Intern

Jul. 2023 - Dec. 2023

DESY & European XFEL, Hamburg, Germany

I contributed to the modelling of diffractive optics for ultrafast X-ray experiments at the European XFEL and DESY, working under the guidance of Dr. Patrik Vagovic, Dr. Tokushi Sato, and Prof. Henry Chapman. My work involved developing a Python implementation of the two-beam case dynamical diffraction theory to support the design and simulation of crystal optics for high-speed X-ray imaging at CFEL/EuXFEL. I also participated in user and in-house beamtime experiments on Megahertz Microscopy within the SPB/SFX instrument at the European XFEL.
